Cauliflower Tomato Soup with Indian Spices

COOKTIME:

1 hour

SEVINGS:

6

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 medium white or yellow onion, chopped/li>
  • 1 28-ounce can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 pound fresh or frozen cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 4 cups water
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup plain, low-fat yogurt, for garnish
  • ½ cup cilantro leaves, for garnish

PREPARATION:

  1. In a 4-quart stock pot combine the canola oil, coriander seeds, mustard seeds, turmeric, cumin seeds, red pepper flakes, and ground coriander. Turn heat to medium and gently warm the spices until you can hear the mustard seeds begin to pop and you begin to smell the heady aroma of the other spices.
  2. Add the onions, reduce the heat to medium-low and cook until the onions are soft, about 5 to 6 minutes.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, cauliflower, and water. Increase the heat to high, bring to a boil, and then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until the cauliflower is soft, about 20 to 25 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and use an immersion blender to create a smooth, “creamy” soup. Season with kosher salt and black pepper.
  5. Garnish, if desired, with cilantro and yogurt, and serve.

NUTRITION FACTS:

Per 1/6 of recipe 130 calories 8 grams of protein 19 grams of carbohydrates 5 grams of fiber 310 milligrams of sodium 745 milligrams of potassium
